An Ethical decision

I used to work at a well-respected university, and during a mid-semester break, we had construction work happening in a section of the campus to avoid disrupting students. On this one particular day while working nearby, despite the construction site being off-limits, I suddenly heard jackhammers and noise come to a halt. It turned out that a worker had made a disrespectful remark to a young female student who happened to be the only student on campus at that time, and all the rest joined in.
This incident struck a chord with me because such behavior was unfortunately not uncommon in the construction industry, and I'd often shrugged it off in the past when in a workshop. But this time, it felt like a significant breach of ethics, especially considering that a university campus should be a safe place for students to walk around.
I made the decision to report the incident to the project management, even though my colleagues didn't see it as a major issue. As a result of my report, one worker was removed from the construction site. Looking back, I'm still conflicted because I may have contributed to someone losing their job. However, I couldn't ignore the fact that this incident crossed a line for me.
In hindsight, I wonder if there could have been a better way to handle the situation, but I acted based on my strong belief that certain boundaries should not be crossed, especially not in what should be a safe place for a lone female student.

Culture and Influence in my life

Values and identity.

My culture and the people around me have played a significant role in shaping my values and identity. Growing up in a divided family environment taught me self-reliance and resilience. The workshop culture I was exposed to during my formative years emphasized humor and camaraderie alongside competitive comparisons.
This environment initially led to self-criticism and a relentless pursuit of self-improvement. However, as I matured, I realized the importance of empathy and breaking free from the notion that my worth was tied to any particular thing. This shift in values was influenced by the evolving perspectives of the people I encountered in my life.
One notable aspect of my evolving identity has been redifining what self-improvement looks like for me. Most recently I've been focused on applying a positive internal dialogue during challenging times, promoting patience and self-acceptance. This for me has been a slow evolution- which ultimately led me to looking for a change of career.

My Strengths

I have a strong aptitude for fault-finding. While I may not always understand the technical intricacies of an issue, I excel at identifying abnormalities and anomalies. I genuinely enjoy reverse engineering problems, which has proven instrumental in overcoming challenges and comprehending complex concepts.

My limitations in my learning and career development.

It's important to acknowledge my limitations as I embark on my learning and career development journey. One of my perceived limitations lies in my information processing speed, particularly concerning technical concepts in the tech industry. I've noticed instances where I took longer to grasp certain ideas compared to my peers, which can be concerning given the fast-paced nature of the field.

How might these affect your learning journey?

This could potentially impact my ability to quickly understand coding and programming concepts, which might require more time and effort on my part. It's easy to fall into the trap of thinking that I might not keep up as efficiently as others.
Furthermore, there's a tendency to let these limitations define my self-image. If I sense that others perceive me as struggling, it could lead to feelings of awkwardness or self-doubt in social interactions.
While I'm mindful of my limitations, I'm also open to the possibility that my assumptions could be flawed. I intend to leverage my strengths, seek assistance when needed, and maintain a growth-oriented mindset. By addressing my limitations constructively, I can approach my learning journey with determination and adaptability, ultimately driving my own progress.

When were you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension.

In a previous job, I faced resistance while implementing a Building Management Systems (BMS) project due to miscommunication. We initially addressed these issues through meetings, but I soon realized that improved communication was essential.
One key reason for the resistance was that our team didn't have a clear idea of the full potential scope we could be working with. To address this, I built a strong rapport with a BMS manager and engaged in discussions to better understand the project's scope. This allowed us to anticipate roadblocks and find solutions, reducing conflicts.
Reflecting on this experience, I see that clear communication is vital. In the future, I'd be more proactive in meetings by asking the right questions for a comprehensive understanding. I'd also encourage team members to voice concerns, emphasizing the importance of sharing perspectives.
Overall, this experience highlighted the significance of communication in productive collaborations. While I managed the situation well, I now recognize that enhanced communication from the start could have prevented some of the resistance and tension in the project.
