My learning plans

My Learning plan for EDA(Bootcamp)

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

Well, my long-term goal hasn't really changed much. I'm aiming to eventually dive into the world of backend or software development. Right now, I'm hoping to kickstart my journey through web development and secure a job in that field. Gradually, I plan to make the shift to backend development. I've realized I'm in my element on some of the more technical aspects of web development, and can stay focused for longer when I'm coding. So I think thats a good track to go down.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

In Bootcamp, I believe my biggest strengths will include:
Engagement with Interesting Content: I tend to stay highly engaged and absorb information efficiently when I find the content interesting and relevant to what I'm working on.
Technical Proficiency: I feel confident in my technical abilities and enjoy working on the technical aspects of coding.
However, I also recognize some limitations:
Attention Span: My attention span tends to be directly correlated with how engaged I am with the content, which can be a challenge when facing less engaging material.
Verbal Learning vs. Reading: I find reading and comprehending abstract or challenging concepts in text documents to be tedious, which may pose a challenge when dealing with complex written documentation or instructions.
Risk of Losing Interest: If I encounter topics or exercises that I don't find particularly interesting, I might invest more time in researching them, potentially slowing down my progress.
In summary, my strengths lie in my engagement with interesting content, technical proficiency, and collaboration skills. However, I need to be mindful of my attention span, potential struggles with abstract or less engaging material, and the risk of losing interest in certain topics.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Public speaking has always made me feel anxious, and the thought of it is quite terrifying. While I would like to improve my confidence in speaking to a group and giving presentations, it's a significant challenge for me. This aspect of the boot camp, working on presentation skills and becoming more comfortable with speaking, is what I anticipate to be my biggest non-technical challenge.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

My expectations of myself during Bootcamp are to fully engage in the learning journey, regardless of my personal preferences or desires. I recognize that sometimes the most valuable learning happens when I step out of my comfort zone and challenge myself. I expect myself to be open to various learning methods and to make the most of the supportive environment provided by the program.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

My expectations of myself during Bootcamp are centered around a proactive and engaged approach to learning. I am committed to getting the most out of each lesson. That means taking personal responsibility for my learning and seeking additional resources or context when necessary.
In a group setting with coordinators and instructors, I expect myself to actively participate by asking questions and seeking insights, especially when it comes to industry standards and practical knowledge. I understand the importance of learning from experienced professionals rather than relying solely on generic or potentially outdated sources.
I also acknowledge the potential time constraints and pressure that may arise during Bootcamp, but I intend to make the most of the resources available, including the support of coordinators and managers. My goal is to use my time efficiently and effectively and to collaborate with others in a meaningful way.
Overall, my expectations for myself on Bootcamp revolve around a proactive, resourceful, and collaborative approach to learning and personal development.

My Learning plan for EDA(foundations)

What is your long-term goal or career pathway?

My long-term goal is to be happy, have potential for career development, and I believe that lies in software development or possibly the backend of web development, sometimes the appeal of frontend can be lost on me with certain aspects . While I know I enjoy optimizing and identifying inefficiencies, which I believe could be valuable in gaming, software development, or backend work within a full-stack application.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning:

I sometimes find it challenging to grasp certain concepts quickly. I learn best through verbal communication rather than reading, where often times videos are more effective for me than written documents. I can focus for hours on topics that genuinely excite me, however with those that don't it can be a tedious task to research. This can be both a strength and a weakness because my ability to absorb information seems to be directly related to my interest in the subject. I'm concerned that I may not absorb information as efficiently as others in the tech industry, which places a high value on rapid learning and adaptability.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to develop my confidence, particularly in communication. I find it nerve-wracking to interact with others, especially in virtual settings where it feels more formal and lacks personal feedback. I've noticed that I'm more at ease in face-to-face interactions and want to bridge this gap. I believe that improving my confidence in remote communication will be crucial for success in the program.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this program:

I commit to being punctual and adhering to a structured learning routine. I will hold myself accountable for starting work by 8:30 in the morning to establish a productive day. Scheduled breaks will allow me to recharge and maintain focus throughout the day. Regarding working with others, I will strive to be available to my classmates and actively engage in collaborative efforts when needed. Communication will be a priority, ensuring my peers are aware of my progress and challenges.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team:

I expect the Facilitation team to be available during working hours to address issues related to coursework or any challenges that may arise. Having their support and guidance readily accessible is crucial for my success in the program.

Any scheduling information:

I do not have any commitments that require my immediate attention. However, I recognize the importance of taking breaks to maintain productivity. Planning my day to prioritize more demanding tasks in the morning and less mentally taxing work in the afternoon aligns with my background in trades and helps me stay productive. Recharging during breaks will be essential for me to produce high-quality work throughout the day.
