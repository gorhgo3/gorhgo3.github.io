Reflecting on core-learnings

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I realized the importance of shifting from a competitive mindset to one where I find inspiration in others' achievements rather than feeling inadequate.
I tend to internalize feedback from others, sometimes turning it into self-doubt. I'm open to being inspired by others but struggle when their success is presented as a challenge to my own progress.
I've observed that I can be overly self-critical and tend to live in my head, especially in group settings, which has become more evident during core learning.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Empathy and Self-Awareness: Empathy and self-awareness play crucial roles in my learning and programming journey. They help me recognize when I'm mentally fatigued and struggling to learn efficiently. It's essential to be empathetic to myself during such times, acknowledging that stress and time constraints can affect my performance. This self-compassion ensures a healthier learning approach. Learning from Others: In programming, I've learned that everyone has their unique problem-solving approach. Reviewing others' code provides insight into their thought process. It's common to feel intimidated, especially when my code seems messy. However, I've embraced the idea that inspiration can come from unexpected places, even in seemingly poorly written code. This openness to learning from others has been valuable.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Core learning is not something typically associated with coding, and I initially thought it might be irrelevant. I found a few key takeaways that resonated with me though. One of them was the concept of neuroplasticity and the importance of recognizing fixed and growth mindsets. Understanding this not only benefits my learning but also my mental well-being.
What stuck with me the most was how some individuals with a fixed mindset felt the need to constantly justify themselves and prove their worth to the world. In contrast, those with a growth mindset are comfortable with themselves and open to being inspired by others. This perspective, especially illustrated through case studies with school children, was a valuable takeaway from the core learning.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging part of the core learning was staying motivated during sections that I didn't feel particularly invested in. Some parts felt less relevant to my coding journey, and I questioned if my time could be better spent coding. During the first and second sprint, when there was a lot of core content, I found it difficult to stay engaged. It's a common experience that not every piece of advice resonates with everyone. I've worked on personal growth throughout my 20s, and certain core concepts align more closely with my experiences, while others felt less applicable.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I believe the emphasis on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course is crucial. In the tech industry, which is fast-paced and competitive, having soft skills and a well-rounded mindset is essential. It sets candidates apart from the crowd and makes them appealing to employers.
Breaking down problems and fault-finding are valuable skills. The ability to retain knowledge, keep an open mind, and maintain a strong sense of self is beneficial. Core learning assists in fostering this mindset.
No matter how skilled one is in technical aspects like JavaScript, CSS, or HTML, there will always be others who are faster or smarter. Recognizing that everyone brings unique strengths and experiences to the table is a key takeaway. It's about what you can offer as a team member who acknowledges both flaws and strengths.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

While I found some sections of the core to be excessive, I understand its purpose. The core learning accommodates a diverse group of learners, and not everything will resonate with everyone. It ensures that everyone has something to reflect upon and learn from. Personally, I believe I could have spent less time on certain core elements, but the parts I resonated with were valuable and have stuck with me. I don't think it was a waste of time, as it provided a well-rounded learning experience.
