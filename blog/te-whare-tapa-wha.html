te-whare-tapa-wha

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

My primary objective is to consistently attend the gym at least three times a week. Recognizing and respecting my body's limits, particularly during bouldering, is crucial. Should I feel any discomfort or potential injury, especially in my fingers, I commit to prioritizing safety over performance and taking a step back. This strategy not only helps prevent overexertion but also significantly reduces the risk of serious injuries. More than just performance metrics, I aim to focus on the quality and intent behind each workout, ensuring both a healthy body and a clear mind.

Embracing the present moment is at the heart of my aspirations. Lately I have been letting some of the relationships with others slip. I wish to reinforce my appreciation for them by cherishing every moment with my loved ones and friends and actively seeking deeper conversations. These are the bonds upon which I've built my life, and which I hold dear. By actively nurturing these relationships, I hope to cultivate an even greater appreciation for those in my life.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

As someone who has grappled with mental health challenges, my commitment remains to continuously engage with my inner self. I aim to practice self-empathy daily, recognizing both my strengths and vulnerabilities. Incorporating daily gratitude allows me to deepen my understanding of myself and gives me the grace to accept that it's okay not to always feel at my best.

Once a week, I will intentionally reflect and remind myself that my worth is not tied to anyone else's perception or the output I produce. My value is intrinsic, and I will nurture that belief.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

I am invested in committing to bolstering my self-awareness and understanding of my own identity. Central to this journey are the practices of gratitude and empathy. I intend to set aside dedicated time and resources to nurture this spiritual growth, ensuring that I remain in tune with my inner self and the world around me.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

I am making a conscious commitment to step outside daily during my study sessions, without any specific agenda. The simple act of feeling the wind, listening to the trees, or distant hum of the streets have a grounding effect. I've realized that I often miss the beauty of good weather by being absorbed in study. Therefore, I aim to prioritize short breaks, especially when the sun is out. As this not only connects me to nature but also brings the added benefit of soaking in some vitamin D from the sunshine.
